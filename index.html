About Me

Driven by a strong academic foundation in financial modeling, statistics, and econometrics, my interests lie in applying quantitative methods to the analysis of financial markets.

My work focuses on Python-based data analysis, time-series modeling, and empirical finance, with an emphasis on translating financial theory into structured, data-driven frameworks.

I am particularly drawn to asset pricing, risk modeling, and quantitative investment strategies, approaching these areas with analytical rigor, intellectual curiosity, and a continuous learning mindset.

My objective is to progressively develop robust quantitative skills while contributing disciplined, methodical thinking in research-oriented financial environments.
